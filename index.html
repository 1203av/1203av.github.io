Special Relativity Theory

Einstein's Special Relativity Theory connects to his most famous equation E = mc2. His equation came to the conclusion that energy and mass can be exchanged. Albert Einstein's theory of special relativity is an explanation of how a change in an object's speed affects measurements of its time, space, and mass. Suppose a juggler was juggling balls in a train. The juggler sees the ball coming at a slow pace. But if they pass by a train station, the person who is on the platform sees the ball going really fast! If the juggler's balls where waves of light, per say, then the person on the platform and the person on the platform and the juggler sees the ball at the same speed.

General Relativity Theory

One of the most famous and difficult concepts that he found was the General Relativity Theory. Einstein spent 10 years working on the General Relativity. He came to the conclusion that massive objects cause a distortion to the space-time continuum.

His Thought Process

In Einstein's theory of Special Relativity, he concluded that physics is the same for all non-accelerating objects. He found that that there is one single continuum called The Space-Time Continuum. Einstein then found out that massive objects, like planets and stars, cause a distortion in The Space-Time Continuum.That is the reason that gravity is there. If you put a large person in the center of a trampoline it creates a dip in the trampoline. Same idea for the Space-Time Continuum.
